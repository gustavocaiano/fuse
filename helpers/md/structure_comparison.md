# Thesis Structure Comparison: Your Thesis vs. Example Thesis

This document compares the structural choices of your current thesis draft (`thesis/main.pdf`) against the example thesis (`helpers/pdf/DIMEI_1190977_v7.pdf`), focusing specifically on the sections you have developed so far.

## 1. Structural Differences (How content is organized)

*   **Project Planning:** 
    *   **You:** Have a dedicated, highly detailed **Chapter 3: Planeamento de Trabalho**.
    *   **Example:** Only has a brief **subsection** inside the Introduction (`Introduction > Project Planning`).
*   **Background vs. State of the Art:**
    *   **You:** Combined your literature review and theoretical background into a single **Chapter 2: Estudo do Estado da Arte**.
    *   **Example:** Split this into two distinct chapters: **Chapter 2: Background on OCR** (for fundamental concepts) and **Chapter 3: State of the Art** (strictly for LRRQs).

## 2. Where You Have MORE (or Deeper Structure)

*   **Introduction - Research Question:** You explicitly define a **"Pergunta de Investigação"** in your Introduction. The example thesis does not have a dedicated subsection for this.
*   **State of the Art - General Process:** You include a general **"Processo de Investigação"** at the very beginning of the State of the Art chapter before diving into specific LRRQs. 
*   **State of the Art - Discussion Depth in TOC:** Your Table of Contents shows a much deeper breakdown of the "Discussão" sections for each LRRQ (e.g., listing specific topics like "Padrões Arquiteturais", "Limitações dos Protocolos Legacy"). The example thesis just leaves it at "Discussion".
*   **Planning Details:** Because you made Planning a full chapter, you have significantly more structural depth here, including "Definição do Âmbito", "Project Charter", "Identificação de Stakeholders", and "Gestão de Riscos". The example thesis lacks these explicit sections.

## 3. What is SIMILAR (Direct Matches)

*   **Introduction Core:** Both of you follow the exact same core flow for the Introduction: 
    *   Context (Contexto)
    *   Problem (Problema)
    *   Objectives (Objetivos)
    *   Methodology (Metodologia)
    *   Ethical Considerations (Considerações Éticas)
    *   Document Structure (Estrutura do Documento)
*   **LRRQ Methodology:** Both theses use the exact same Literature Review Research Question (LRRQ) framework for the State of the Art, breaking each question down into "Search Process" (Processo de Pesquisa) and "Discussion" (Discussão).
*   **Appendices:** Both make use of Appendices to attach external documents (you attached your Questionnaire and Project Charter; the example attached test datasets).

## 4. Where the Example Has MORE (or Deeper Structure)

*   **Frontmatter Lists:** 
    *   **Example:** Includes a "List of Source Code" and a "List of Acronyms".
    *   **You:** Currently only have "Lista de Figuras" and "Lista de Tabelas". (You may want to add a List of Acronyms).
*   **Conclusions Breakdown:**
    *   **Example:** Breaks the Conclusions down into specific reflective subsections: "Document summary", "Achieved objectives", "Challenges and future work", and "Final appreciation".
    *   **You:** Have a simpler structure with just "Conclusões" and "Trabalho Futuro".
*   **State of the Art Consistency:**
    *   **Example:** Strictly uses the LRRQ format for the entire State of the Art chapter.
    *   **You:** Use LRRQ1 and LRRQ2, but then switch to a standard topical review for "Estado da Arte em Visão Computacional" (which has phases 1, 2, 3 instead of a Search Process/Discussion format).
